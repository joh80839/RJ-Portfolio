My Favorite NBA Teams for the 2025-2026 Season

This is a collection of six teams that I like to watch for the 2025-2026 NBA season.

San Antonio, Spurs

This year's Spurs team is like no other, whether we are comparing them to the old Spurs during the Tim Duncan days or just other teams right now, this team is something we've never seen before. Spurs have a great duo and the people have been calling it "Area 51". The "five" is for Stephon Castle, a 2nd-year player who won Rookie of the Year last year, has been ecstatic, and has been performing dunks that will get your grandmother out her seat. The "one" is for the potential all-time great, Victor Wembanyama, who is another Rookie of the Year and can do anything he wants on the court. The man can handle the ball, shoot 3's, fadeaways, windmill dunks, reverse dunks; you name it! He has been on a roll! The only thing that can stop him is himself.

Houston, Rockets

If all things go well, this team can honestly be a team that could go to the 2026 NBA Finals; if it's not the finals, then it's definitely the Western Conference Finals. This is one of the most athletically gifted teams right now. Amen Thompson can go from being on the ground to being in the air so quick, you would think this man was part rabbit. They don't have too many "flat out scorers", but they do have one, and his name is Kevin Durant, a bona fide mid-range scorer and a first ballot Hall of Famer. When times get tough, Kevin Durant can bail the team out and win games like no other.

Los Angeles, Lakers

If you've never seen a magician, I can tell you the name of one; his first name is Luka and his last name is Doncic. Luka Doncic has been playing out of his mind and has been averaging more than 30 points a game. He's putting up Michael Jordan numbers, people! Even with the first ballot Hall of Famer, Lebron James, being out, Luka can find a way to make magic happen and win games. Even if Luka is not playing, the Lakers can find ways to win with Austin Reaves, who still continues to surprise people with what he can do when he is a starter.

Golden State, Warriors

This is not the Golden State Warriors of 2015-2016 season, also known as the 73-9 season, which is still the best NBA regular season record of all time, but they still show flashes of it. When Stephen Curry is healthy, it is "Must-see TV!". All it takes is just one three to go in and he will just keep them coming with no mercy. When he is not healthy, Jimmy Butler and friends can find ways to close games out.

Oklahoma, Thunder

These are the 2025 NBA Champions, and they are looking to win it again this year to create what they call a "two-peat". I like this young group of guys. They are one of the youngest teams in the NBA, and they play every game like it's their last. They have the most recent NBA MVP, Shai. He makes getting 30 points easy, which is extremely hard to do in the NBA, the best professional basketball league in the world. Let's see if they can win a finals again. If they can, I have nothing but respect for them.

New York, Knicks

The Knicks were close to going to the finals last season by making it to the Eastern Conference Finals; it would have been their first finals in almost 30 years if they had made it. Jalen Brunson is still a top guard in the NBA and without a doubt in the Eastern Conference. Karl Anthony-Towns and Jalen have what it takes to go to the Finals, if they keep doing what they did last year. Their playing style is good and it can send them to the Hall of Fame, if they can stay healthy, of course.
